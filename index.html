about

I am a fifth year PhD student at Stanford University, and I use computation and modeling to gain insight into the structure of materials and the nature of chemical reactions. I received my BS in chemical engineering and business, economics, and management from Caltech in 2015. My primary interests like in computationally modeling chemical reactions and the intersection of computer vision techniques and transmission electron microscopy.


image analysis and microscopy

Throughout my PhD, I have spent over 1000 hours understanding the nanostructure of materials via image analysis and transmission electron microscopy. Past collaborators have included National Center for Electron Microscopy and ThermoFischer Scientific. Through combining computer vision and transmission electron microscopy, I hope to teach computers how to identify the atomic structure of materials and molecules.
